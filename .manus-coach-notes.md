# Trading Coach — example setup conventions (from 41 reference screenshots)

All examples are TradingView charts by user "xilitidisioannis", FXCM forex pairs.

## Visual conventions observed
- Timeframe: mostly **1h (H1)**, some **4h (H4)**. Header shows e.g. "Euro / Swiss Franc · 1h · FXCM".
- Indicators always present: **EMA 50/100** (single black/grey line shown as "EMA 50/100"), and **RSI (14, close)** subpanel with its own MA (purple = RSI, yellow = RSI MA).
- Pairs seen: NZDCHF, USDCAD, EURCHF, CHFJPY, USDJPY (majors + crosses, often CHF/JPY crosses).
- **Green rectangles** = POI zones (supply/demand / support & resistance boxes). Usually two: last support and previous resistance (or vice-versa).
- **Long position tool** = green/teal shaded box projecting right = target (TP) zone above entry; **red shaded box** below = stop-loss zone. (Standard TradingView long position drawing: green to target, red to SL.)
- **Short** = mirror: green target box below, red SL box above.
- Some charts annotate **Elliott waves** with (1)(2)(3)(4)(5) labels and a blue zig-zag projection line (e.g. USDCAD one projects wave 5 down → short bias).
- Economic-news markers (flag icons) sometimes shown on the time axis (USD/CAD flags) → confirms "check for news".
- "Replay" watermark on some = chart was in bar-replay/backtest mode.

## The rubric (user's spoken instructions) — 10 criteria + checklist
1. Trend must exist (clear directional bias).
2. Multi-timeframe agreement: analyse on H1, H1 and H4 must AGREE.
3. Breakout + retest: break a support (then retest) for shorts, or break a resistance (then retest) for longs. POIs placed at last support & previous resistance.
4. EMA50 confirmation on BOTH H1 & H4: for LONG the candle must have broken/closed ABOVE EMA50; for SHORT closed BELOW EMA50.
5. Stop loss: LONG → below last support (+ a bit of the wick); SHORT → above last resistance (+ a bit of the wicks).
6. Elliott waves: ideally aligns with wave 4→5.
7. Risk/Reward: minimum 1:2.
8. News check: avoid high-impact news on the chosen pair or broad market-moving news.
9. Timing: trade Tue/Wed/Thu (sometimes Fri), mainly **New York session 14:00–18:00** (user's local UTC+3 charts).
10. Must align with the existing 20-point Pre-Trade Checklist.
